Courses After 12th

Explore various undergraduate programs and vocational courses available after 12th grade.

B.Tech (Engineering)

B.Tech is an undergraduate degree in engineering, which provides in-depth knowledge and skills in various engineering disciplines.

Specializations:

  • Computer Science Engineering

  • Civil Engineering
  • Electrical Engineering
  • Electronics and Communication Engineering

Subjects:

  • Mathematics
  • Physics
  • Chemistry
  • Engineering Drawing
  • Technical Communication

Future Paths:

  • Higher Studies (M.Tech, M.S.)
  • Professional Certifications (PMP, Six Sigma)

MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery)

MBBS is an undergraduate medical degree required to become a doctor. It typically spans five and a half years, including one year of compulsory internship. The course covers various disciplines of medicine, surgery, and human anatomy.

Subjects:

  • Anatomy
  • Biochemistry
  • Physiology
  • Pathology
  • Pharmacology
  • Microbiology
  • Forensic Medicine
  • Community Medicine
  • General Medicine
  • Surgery
  • Pediatrics
  • Obstetrics and Gynecology

Future Paths:

  • General Practitioner (GP)
  • Specialist Doctor (e.g., Cardiologist, Neurologist)
  • Surgeon
  • Medical Researcher
  • Public Health Professional

Preparation Tips:

Focus on Biology and Chemistry during 11th and 12th grade. Prepare for medical entrance exams like NEET, AIIMS, and state-level medical entrance exams. Engage in internships and hospital training during the course.

B.Des (Bachelor of Design)

B.Des is an undergraduate degree in design that covers various aspects of design, including visual communication, product design, and interaction design. It aims to develop creativity and technical skills in designing solutions for real-world problems.

Specializations:

  • Graphic Design
  • Product Design
  • Interaction Design
  • Interior Design
  • Fashion Design

Subjects:

  • Design Fundamentals
  • Visual Communication
  • Typography
  • Color Theory
  • Product Design
  • Human-Centered Design
  • 3D Modeling
  • Design Research

Future Paths:

  • Graphic Designer
  • Product Designer
  • UX/UI Designer
  • Design Researcher
  • Design Consultant
  • Entrepreneur in Design

Preparation Tips:

Develop a strong portfolio showcasing your design projects. Focus on art and design fundamentals in school. Prepare for entrance exams and interviews by practicing design challenges and staying updated with design trends.

BBA (Bachelor of Business Administration)

BBA is an undergraduate degree in business management that provides a broad understanding of business operations and management principles. It prepares students for leadership roles and entrepreneurial ventures in various industries.

Specializations:

  • Marketing
  • Finance
  • Human Resource Management
  • International Business
  • Operations Management

Subjects:

  • Business Mathematics
  • Financial Accounting
  • Principles of Management
  • Marketing Management
  • Organizational Behavior
  • Business Economics
  • Human Resource Management
  • Business Law
  • Strategic Management

Future Paths:

  • Business Manager
  • Marketing Executive
  • Financial Analyst
  • Human Resource Manager
  • Entrepreneur
  • Consultant

Preparation Tips:

Develop strong analytical and communication skills. Gain proficiency in business-related software and tools. Prepare for entrance exams if applicable, and engage in internships or part-time jobs to gain practical experience in business settings.

Subjects:

  • Hotel Operations Management

  • Food Production
  • Hospitality Law
  • Human Resource Management
  • Financial Management

Future Paths:

  • Hotel Manager
  • Restaurant Manager
  • Event Coordinator
  • Food and Beverage Director
  • Tourism Manager
  • Hospitality Consultant

Preparation Tips:

Gain practical experience through internships in hotels and restaurants. Develop strong communication and customer service skills. Stay updated on industry trends and standards. Prepare for entrance exams if applicable, and focus on hospitality-related coursework.

B.Pharm (Bachelor of Pharmacy)

B.Pharm is an undergraduate degree in pharmacy that prepares students for careers in pharmaceutical sciences. The program covers the study of drugs, their effects, and their interactions, as well as the principles of pharmaceutical practice.

Specializations:

  • Pharmaceutics
  • Pharmaceutical Chemistry
  • Pharmacology
  • Pharmacognosy
  • Clinical Pharmacy

Subjects:

  • Pharmaceutical Chemistry
  • Pharmaceutics
  • Pharmacology
  • Pharmacognosy
  • Biochemistry
  • Microbiology
  • Pharmacy Practice
  • Drug Store Management
  • Clinical Research

Future Paths:

  • Pharmacist
  • Clinical Research Associate
  • Pharmaceutical Sales Representative
  • Drug Inspector
  • Pharmaceutical Scientist
  • Quality Control Analyst

Preparation Tips:

Focus on subjects like Chemistry and Biology during 11th and 12th grade. Prepare for entrance exams if applicable. Gain practical experience through internships in pharmacies or pharmaceutical companies. Stay updated on industry trends and advancements in drug development.

BSW (Bachelor of Social Work)

BSW is an undergraduate degree focused on preparing students for professional practice in social work. It covers topics related to human behavior, social policies, and community welfare, equipping students to support individuals and communities in need.

Specializations:

  • Child and Family Welfare
  • Community Development
  • Health and Mental Health
  • Criminal Justice and Corrections
  • Gerontology

Subjects:

  • Introduction to Social Work
  • Human Behavior and the Social Environment
  • Social Work Practice with Individuals and Families
  • Social Work Practice with Groups and Communities
  • Social Policy and Administration
  • Research Methods in Social Work
  • Ethics and Professionalism in Social Work
  • Fieldwork and Practicum

Future Paths:

  • Social Worker
  • Community Organizer
  • Case Manager
  • Clinical Social Worker
  • Policy Analyst
  • Program Coordinator
  • Social Work Educator

Preparation Tips:

Gain experience through internships or volunteer work in social services or community organizations. Develop strong communication and empathetic skills. Stay informed about social issues and policies. Prepare for entrance exams if applicable, and focus on subjects related to human behavior and social sciences.
